Right now, you're the copy-paste machine

When you want your AI to use something you're looking at, you do it by hand — and it barely works.

Today — copy & paste
  • Switch to the tab, select all, copy, paste — for every page.
  • One page at a time. Six tabs means six trips.
  • You paste the menus and clutter along with the content.
  • It's gone the moment the page changes.
  • Work docs behind a login, designs, PDFs — you're stuck.

  • Just say "read the doc I'm looking at." It's done.
  • Point at several tabs at once and ask across all of them.
  • It pulls the clean content — no menus, no clutter.
  • It stays put — nothing to copy, nothing to lose.

What you can finally do

Whatever you have open, your AI can read it — in plain language, no copy-paste, even the documents behind your work login.

📄 Use the doc you're reading

"Summarize this 40-page spec." "What are the open questions in this doc?" "Pull the action items out of this thread." Ask about whatever's on your screen — it reads the whole thing so you don't have to.

🔒 Use your work documents

The Google Doc, the SharePoint file, the internal wiki, the dashboard behind your company sign-in. Your AI reads them the same way you do — already logged in — instead of hitting a login screen.

🎨 Designs, PDFs and sheets too

"What does this Figma show?" "Pull the numbers from this PDF." "What's in this budget sheet?" The visual things your AI usually can't open, it now can.

🔎 Across everything you've got open

"Answer this from the six articles I have open." "Which of my tabs talked about pricing?" It reads and compares all of them at once — and tells you which page each answer came from.

🗣️ Just say what you mean

Refer to a tab the way you'd say it out loud — "the figma one," "the doc I'm looking at," "what's on my screen." No URLs, no codes. It figures out which one.

📌 Keep your research

Save the set of pages you were working through, and pull them back days later — even after the tabs are long closed — so your AI can pick up where you left off.

How it works

Three steps, and you only do the first one. Handle reads what's on your screen, right on your computer — your AI does the thinking.

01 · point

Point at a tab

Say it the way you'd say it out loud — "the doc I'm reading," "the figma one," "what's on my screen." No URLs, no codes.

02 · read

It reads it

Handle pulls the real content off that page — even your work documents behind a login — and hands it over clean, without the menus and clutter.

03 · use

Your AI uses it

Now it's in the conversation. Summarize it, ask questions about it, compare it across tabs, turn it into a task — whatever you'd do next.

You just say it, in plain English:
  • "Summarize the doc I'm looking at."
  • "What does the figma show?"
  • "Answer this from the articles I have open."
  • "Which of my tabs talked about pricing?"

Set it up once

You'll need macOS, Google Chrome, and an AI assistant you already use (like Claude Code). It's free and open source, runs entirely on your own computer — no account, no API key. It reads your tabs; it never sends them anywhere, and it can't touch anyone else's.

1

Get it

git clone https://github.com/abhitsian/handle.git ~/claude-apps/handle
2

Connect it to your AI

One line, and your assistant can read your tabs:

claude mcp add handle -- node ~/claude-apps/handle/mcp/server.js
3

Let it see your pages

The first time, your Mac and Chrome will ask permission for Handle to read your open tabs — say yes. That's what lets it read what you're looking at, including the pages behind your work login.

4

Just talk to it

"Summarize the doc I'm looking at."   "What does the figma show?"   "Answer this from my open tabs."   That's the whole thing.
